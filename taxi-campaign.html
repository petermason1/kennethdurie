Campaign for Free or Heavily Discounted Taxis for Disabled People

Join our campaign to make transportation more accessible and affordable for disabled people. Together, we can make a difference.

About the Campaign

Transportation is a fundamental right that enables independence, access to healthcare, employment, and social participation. Many disabled people face significant barriers and costs when it comes to getting around. This campaign advocates for free or heavily discounted taxi services for disabled people, ensuring everyone has access to safe, reliable, and affordable transportation.

Write to Your Local MP

Use the letter template below to contact your local MP and advocate for free or heavily discounted taxi services for disabled people. Personalise it with your own experiences and circumstances.

Letter Template

[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Your Postcode]
[Date]

[MP's Name]
[MP's Address]

Dear [MP's Name],

I am writing to you as a constituent to raise an important issue regarding transportation accessibility for disabled people in our area.

Transportation is essential for disabled people to access healthcare appointments, employment opportunities, education, and social activities. However, many disabled people face significant financial barriers when it comes to getting around, particularly when public transport is not accessible or available.

I am supporting a campaign calling for free or heavily discounted taxi services for disabled people. This would:

  • Improve access to essential services and healthcare
  • Enable greater independence and social participation
  • Reduce isolation and improve quality of life
  • Support disabled people in accessing employment and education
  • Provide a reliable alternative when public transport is not accessible

[Add your personal experience here - how transportation barriers affect you or someone you know]

I would be grateful if you could:

  • Raise this issue in Parliament
  • Support initiatives for discounted or free taxi services for disabled people
  • Work with local authorities to explore funding options for such a scheme
  • Meet with disability organisations to discuss this matter further

I look forward to hearing your views on this important matter and would welcome the opportunity to discuss it further with you.

Yours sincerely,

[Your Name]
